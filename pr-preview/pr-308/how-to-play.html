How to Play

Controls, the objective, and the moment-to-moment battle loop.
Published

Last updated: 2026-06-29

You command the blue army (top of the field). Your objective: defeat the red army (bottom). A side loses when it has no units left fighting — every remaining unit is dead or routing.

Controls

Action Control
Select a unit Left-click its block or its raised flag
Select multiple Left-click and drag a box
Move Right-click the ground
Form up Right-click and drag open ground (left flank → right flank); with several units selected, the line is split among them
Form up in selection order Shift + right-click and drag (instead of the default left-to-right field order)
Form-up split mode Y cycles equal-depth ↔︎ equal-width (set the default in ☰ Menu)
Attack Right-click an enemy unit
Add waypoint Shift + Right-click (chain several move points)
Order stance H hold · F flank · R rear · K skirmish · G support · Esc clear
Formation T (cycles Normal / Tight / Loose)
Line width [ narrower · ] wider, or drag a flank grip
Pan camera WASD / arrow keys / screen edges
Zoom Mouse wheel
Show all orders hold Space
Active pause P (or Shift + Space)

A right-click on empty ground is a move order; a right-click on an enemy is an attack order. Selection and camera stay live even while paused, so you can plan under pressure.

Zoom in to see the soldiers. From the usual overview each regiment is a block of small marks. Zoom in far enough and those marks become individual figures, and each type carries its own gear — spearmen shoulder a spear, archers carry a bow, other foot raise a shield, and cavalry ride a mount that faces the regiment’s march. So you can watch the ranks as people, not dots, and still read who is who. Zoom back out and they collapse to the legible marks again.

Zoom in on a melee and the front-rank fighters work their weapons — thrusting and swinging their blades at the enemy on the attack cadence. The blade length tracks each unit’s reach, so a spear line visibly out-thrusts a sword line: the longest weapons reach furthest toward the foe (see Weapon reach).

Order stances. Beyond plain move/attack you can arm a stance with a hotkey before you click; a coloured cursor shows which is active, and Esc clears it.

  • Hold (H) — stand ground; don’t chase a detected enemy.
  • Attack flank (F) / Attack rear (R) — maneuver to strike the enemy’s side or back, where hits land far harder (see Tactics).
  • Skirmish (K) — a ranged unit keeps its distance, backing off when a threat closes instead of standing to fire.
  • Support (G) — right-click a friendly to guard it: the unit shadows that ally and engages anything that threatens it.

Press T to cycle a selected unit’s formation — Tight closes ranks for better missile and charge defence, Loose spreads out, Normal is the default.

Resize the line. A regiment normally forms up at a sensible width, but you can reshape it: press ] to widen the line by one file (shallower, more men fighting at the front) or [ to narrow it (deeper, fewer up front but more staying power). With a single unit selected, two small grips appear on its flanks — drag a grip in or out to set the width directly, with a live preview of the new file count. The width holds as the unit takes casualties, and shows in the info panel.

Deploy with a drag (form up). With a single unit selected, right-click and drag across open ground to lay out where it should stand: the start of the drag is the unit’s left flank, the end is its right flank. The unit marches over and wheels to face the line — the drag sets its position, width, and facing in one gesture, with a live preview of the front line and a forward arrow. A short right-click (no drag) is still a plain move.

Deploy several units at once. Select multiple units first, then drag the same flank line: it’s split into one contiguous slice per unit, with a small gap between them. By default the line splits to equal depth — every regiment forms up the same number of ranks, so a bigger unit simply takes a wider slice and the whole front is a uniform wall. Press Y to switch to equal width (every unit the same frontage, so a small unit just stacks deeper) and back; the choice you make in ☰ Menu → Form-up is the default each battle starts with. By default the units fill the line left-to-right by where they already stand, so each marches to the nearest slice without crossing a neighbour; hold Shift while you drag to place them in selection order instead (the first unit you picked takes the left flank). The preview shows every unit’s slice, file count, and the active split mode before you release.

The battle loop

  1. Select — left-click a unit, or drag a box to grab several at once. The info panel fills with the selected unit’s stats.
  2. Order — right-click to send them. Move them onto an enemy’s flank, or right-click the enemy directly to attack. There is a brief reaction delay (~0.5 s, faster for cavalry) before units execute — orders aren’t instant.
  3. Watch the bars — each unit flies a small regimental flag in its team colour and shows a strength bar that drops as it takes casualties. The flag is a click target too: clicking it selects the unit, just like clicking the block. Morale matters as much as numbers (see Tactics).
  4. Navigate terrain — the field has two terrain patches: a dark-green forest (slows movement to 60%) on the left flank, and a tan hill (impassable) on the right. Units route around the hill automatically. See Tactics → Terrain.
  5. Exploit the break — when a unit routs, it flees (shown faded) and stops counting toward the battle. Routs spread to nearby allies, so a single break can cascade.
  6. Win — eliminate or rout the entire enemy army and a Victory overlay appears, with Fight Again to restart.

Campaign Map

Sparta includes a turn-based campaign layer on top of its tactical battles. Select a scenario from the Main Menu to open the campaign map.

On your turn:

  1. Click one of your provinces (highlighted when you have an army there).
  2. Click an adjacent province to move, or attack an enemy one if you are at war.
  3. Press End Turn when you are done. Each AI faction takes its turn automatically.

Diplomacy: the right-hand panel lists every surviving rival. Click Declare War or Sue for Peace to change your stance. Suing for peace sets a truce that prevents re-declaring war for a few turns.

Rulers and AI personality: each faction is led by a named ruler whose trait shapes its diplomacy. The turn banner at top-left names the current faction’s ruler in parentheses (for example, Rome’s banner reads “Rome (Julius Caesar)”).

Trait War threshold Peace threshold
Normal Attacks at 1.5× strength advantage Sues for peace below 0.6×
Aggressive Attacks at 1.25× advantage Fights longer (sues below 0.33×)
Defensive Needs a 2× advantage to attack Sues for peace sooner (below 0.8×)

Province counts appear in the standings line below the banner. Own every province to win the campaign.

Recording & replays

Every battle is recorded automatically — a ● REC indicator shows top-center. When a battle ends it’s saved, and you can:

  • Watch Replay — re-run the battle you just played (▶ REPLAY). You can still pan and inspect units during playback, but can’t issue new orders.
  • Load Replay — open any earlier saved battle from a file picker (top-right, also on the end screen).
  • Fight Again — start a fresh, newly-recorded battle.

See Replays for how the deterministic system works and why the logs are tiny.
